Back to work: the six-month catch-up

You have been away for a while, and half a year of email, Teams and documents is waiting. One giant prompt will not read it all. This walks you through it calmly, one manageable piece at a time, using the upgraded Microsoft 365 Copilot that can see your work data.

Use the upgraded Microsoft 365 Copilot that can see your email, Teams and work files. Start a New Chat for every run.
The idea

Why one big prompt fails

It feels natural to type "summarise everything I missed and tell me what to do". It rarely works. Copilot does not read your whole mailbox at once, it searches it and works from a slice of what it finds. Ask for too much and the quiet, important things slip through. Here is what is really going on, in plain terms.

📥 It only reads the top of the pile

Search hands back a limited set of matches, ranked by relevance, not by date. A quiet one-to-one message where a task was handed to you can be dropped entirely.

🧠 Even a big memory blurs

Modern models hold a lot at once, but pour six months of email and transcripts in together and the detail smears. Precise answers need a smaller pile.

🎲 Ask twice, get two answers

AI is not a calculator. The same prompt run twice gives slightly different results, so a single pass can silently miss a real task.

⏳ Your oldest data may be expiring

Some organisations delete old Teams chats or email on a schedule (many do not). If yours does, your earliest month is the most at risk, so do it first and check with IT if unsure.

The fix is two habits: chunk the job into pieces small enough to read properly, and verify by running the important pieces twice and merging them. The rest of this page builds that workflow around your details.

Step 1

Set up your run

Fill these in and every prompt below fills itself in with your details. Nothing is sent anywhere, it all stays in your browser.

Chunk by

One card per month across your leave, oldest first. Best when you are not sure what you missed.

How thorough

Run each chunk twice in fresh chats, then merge the two tables. Slower, but it catches what one pass drops.

Not sure? Leave it on Time and Run twice + merge. That is the safe default: thorough, and it double-checks itself.

Step 2

Pick your mode

Copilot lets you choose how hard it thinks. Match the mode to the job.

🔎 Extract with the deep thinker

For the month-by-month searches, pick the deepest thinking mode (in Copilot this is GPT-5.4 Think Deeper). It is slower, but it actually reads what it finds, so it misses less.

⚡ Merge with the fast one

For merging two tables you already have, switch to a fast Instant mode. The data is in front of it, so speed beats depth.

🧼 Fresh chat every run

Start a New Chat for each run and each month. Copilot's memory can carry parts of one answer into the next, which quietly muddles a careful catch-up.

Model names change often. If you do not see these exact names, the rule still holds: deepest mode to dig, fastest mode to merge.

Step 3

Extract, one chunk at a time

Each card below is a ready-to-copy prompt for a single chunk. Work through them in order.

Order matters
Start with your oldest month and work forwards. If your organisation deletes old data on a schedule, the earliest month is the first to disappear.
Step 4

Merge and verify

You ran each chunk twice. Now let Copilot combine the two tables into one you can trust.

Open a fresh chat and switch to the fast Instant mode. Paste both tables into the prompt below where shown. Because the tables are already there, this step is quick.

Step 5

Build your catch-up Notebook

Gather everything you have checked into one Copilot Notebook, so the last steps work from that clean set instead of your whole network.

Open Copilot Notebooks m365.cloud.microsoft/notebooks ↗
Make one notebook for your whole return.
  1. Create one new notebook for your return (call it something like "Back to work").
  2. Paste each verified, merged table into it as a Copilot Page, so your whole timeline lives in one place.
  3. Add the key files you missed as references (Word, PowerPoint, Excel, PDF or OneNote). Adding a file directly guarantees Copilot uses it. If you point it at a whole SharePoint folder instead, it uses up to the 300 most relevant files.
Why this matters
Once your material is in the notebook, Copilot answers only from what you added. That is what removes the guesswork: no trawling the wider network, no drifting onto unrelated projects.
Optional: find the exact files you missed
Not sure which files to add? Ask full Copilot to find the documents that changed while you were away, then add the important ones to your notebook. These search your whole Microsoft 365, so run them in a normal Copilot chat, not inside the notebook.

Step 6

Brainstorm your first weeks back

Now you have a checked picture of what you missed, turn it into a plan for the days ahead.

Run this as a chat inside your catch-up Notebook, so it works only from your verified tables and the files you added, and cites them.

Step 7

Turn it into something you can use

Your notebook is now a private, checked workspace. Ask it to reshape that into whatever helps you most.

Use Quick Create in the notebook to draft a Word "return plan" or a PowerPoint update for your manager straight from your data. Three more that fit easing back in:

🎧 Audio overview

Ask for a podcast-style summary of your notebook and listen to your catch-up on the commute, no reading required.

🗺️ Mind map

Turn the projects, gaps and people into a visual map so you can see the whole picture at a glance.

📊 Excel action tracker

Have the Excel agent draft a task list from your notebook, so the plan becomes something you can work through.

Good habits

Stay safe, go at your pace

✅ Keep yourself in the loop

Every row has a source link. Open a few and read the original before you act on anything that matters.

🔒 Keep work data in Copilot

For anything confidential, stay in Microsoft 365 Copilot rather than pasting it into a public chatbot.

🧭 Fresh chat, oldest first

New chat for each run, and start with your earliest month so nothing ages out before you reach it.

🌱 Go at your pace

This is a catch-up, not a race. One or two chunks a day is fine. It is completely normal to still be finding your feet.
