University AI Position

Our Overarching AI Position

The University sees Artificial Intelligence (AI) as a potentially useful tool to augment human capabilities, enhance innovation and advance our mission whilst maintaining human agency as central to everything we do. We also recognise that AI poses risks, including to critical skills development, and must be approached with appropriate caution and safeguards.

Core Principles

(Education, Research and Workplace Productivity)

1
Human-First Approach
AI enhances rather than replaces human judgement, creativity and critical thinking across our bilingual community
2
Responsible AI Use
We augment capabilities through transparent and accountable AI adoption with robust data protection
3
Continuous Learning
We support building AI literacy and adopt a community approach to learning, recognising that due to the rapid development of AI as a technology we need collaborative spaces for conversations, safe experimentation and shared learning

This position serves as our foundational starting point. From this foundation, our three key areas will develop specific guidance and practices that align with these principles whilst addressing their unique contexts and needs.

This overarching position provides high-level guidance across the university to ensure institutional clarity and avoid fragmented AI adoption across Research, Education, and Workplace Productivity. It will be reviewed regularly given the rapid pace of AI advancement.
