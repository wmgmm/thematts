AI in the Workplace Workshop: Learning Outcomes

AI as Your Co-Pilot: Start AI upskilling with "The Matts"

Curious about leveraging AI responsibly in your work in a human centric way or confused and don't know where to start? Join the conversation with Dr Matthew Mort and Matthew Hayden for a practical 'AI in the Workplace' session. Get a taste of how to work alongside AI as a collaborative assistant (co-creation), with a focus on responsible AI use and keeping your data safe. The Matts understand that adopting AI is more about mindset than technology. They've helped staff transform AI skepticism into practical innovation through their 'learn by doing' approach. Cut through the hype and start your AI journey with guides who've been exactly where you are.

  • Explore AI as your workplace assistant
  • Develop good habits for responsible AI use
  • Discover smart ways to blend AI into your work

Access to smartphone or laptop required for Microsoft Copilot Access (4 hour session)

After completing the "AI as Your Co-Pilot" workshop with Dr. Matthew Mort and Matthew Hayden ("The Matts"), participants will be able to:

Learn by Doing

  • Start experimenting with AI implementation using the "give it a go, find small changes, reflect and repeat" approach
  • Experiment with AI tools through guided hands-on exercises to build practical confidence
  • Apply the iterative "rinse, reflect and repeat" methodology to continuously improve AI usage

Workplace Productivity

  • Analyse workplace tasks suitable for AI and tasks AI is not good at
  • Apply AI as a co-creative assistant rather than a replacement for human judgement
  • Distinguish between tasks to automate and those requiring human insight and creativity

Prompt Engineering

  • Develop effective prompts using the five key principles: direction, format, examples, evaluation, and dividing labour
  • Create structured requests including role prompting, output formatting, and constraint specification
  • Improve AI outputs through targeted evaluation and refinement techniques

Responsible AI Use

Protecting Your Data:

  • Identify sensitive data types that should never be shared with AI systems
  • Use appropriate IT-approved tools (like Microsoft Copilot) for workplace tasks
  • Understand the data privacy implications of different AI platforms and services
  • Understand at a high level basic data governance practices to safeguard personal and organisational information
  • Recognise potential biases and limitations in AI-generated content
  • Understand the environmental, ethical and financial considerations of AI implementation

Demos of Common Tools

  • Experience practical demonstrations of industry-standard AI tools
  • Understand key features and appropriate use cases for various AI applications
  • Apply best practices for tool selection based on specific workplace requirements
  • Recognise responsible usage guidelines for different AI platforms
