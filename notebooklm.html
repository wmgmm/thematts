Two kinds of prompt, and they stack. A Content prompt decides what your output says; a Style prompt decides how it looks and sounds. Use a Style prompt on its own, or paste it after a content prompt and you steer both in one shot. Either way it goes in the same place: the Studio revise (pencil) panel, for slides, infographics or video.
Tone steering really works, and it is the fun part. A Style prompt does more than swap colours and fonts; it casts a voice. Ask for a wildlife-documentary narrator or a noir detective and NotebookLM stays in character the whole way through, holding one metaphor across an entire video overview. You are not choosing a font, you are casting a narrator: pick the character and the AI keeps the performance up from the first line to the last.
