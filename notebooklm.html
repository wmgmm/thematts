Important: At Cardiff University, NotebookLM is approved for all confidential (C1/C2) data only when you access it through your CU account (cardiff.ac.uk). In any other organisation, treat it as a personal learning tool and keep to public, non-confidential information unless that organisation has approved its own NotebookLM setup.
Two kinds of prompt. Content prompts decide what your output says; Style prompts decide how it looks and sounds. Use a Style prompt on its own, or paste it after a content prompt to get both at once. Apply either in the Studio revise (pencil) panel: slides, infographics or video.
Tone steering really works. A Style prompt doesn't just swap colours and fonts, it sets a voice. Ask for a wildlife-documentary narrator or a noir detective and NotebookLM stays in character the whole way through, holding one metaphor across an entire video overview. Less like choosing a font, more like casting a narrator: pick the character and the AI keeps the performance up from start to finish.
