Career Development Exercises

Use these AI-powered prompts to explore career opportunities, improve your interview skills, craft compelling applications, and optimise your CV for applicant tracking systems.

Exercise 1: Which Direction Do You Want to Go?

Discover potential career paths you might not have considered based on your existing skills and interests. This prompt acts as a career coach to help you explore new opportunities.

The Prompt:

Act as a career coach. Based on my skills in [Skill 1], [Skill 2], and my interest in [Industry/Topic], what are 5 potential job titles I might not have considered? For each, explain why it's a good fit.

How to use: Replace [Skill 1], [Skill 2], and [Industry/Topic] with your own details. For example: "Based on my skills in data analysis, public speaking, and my interest in healthcare..."

Exercise 2: The Virtual Interview Gym - Master STAR

AI is becoming a popular method to improve interview and communication skills due to its 'safe space' nature. Use AI as your simulator and coach to practice the STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Important: Use the structure, not the script. Do NOT memorise AI-generated answers. The goal is to practise the STAR framework and develop your own authentic responses.

Setup Prompt (Run This First):

You are an expert interviewer. Conduct a mock interview by asking three questions in total. Ask one question at a time.

After you've answered the interview questions, use this coaching prompt:

Coaching Prompt (Run After Interview):

Act as my interview coach. Analyse my previous answers using STAR. Grade Situation, Task, Action, Result. Rewrite as a model response.

STAR Framework:

  • Situation: Set the context
  • Task: Explain your role
  • Action: What you did
  • Result: The outcome
Exercise 3: Struggling with a Blank Page When Writing a Cover Letter

This prompt helps you overcome the blank page problem by asking you thoughtful questions first, then using your answers to draft a professional cover letter. The AI will wait for your responses before creating the draft.

The Prompt:

Act as a helpful writing assistant. I'm a UK university employee applying for the role of [INSERT ROLE] at [INSERT ORGANISATION]. I need you to help me draft a professional cover letter (approx. 300 words). To do this, you MUST first generate and ask me two key questions that will help you gather the most useful information for the letter. Once you have asked your two questions, wait for my answers. Then, use my responses to draft the cover letter.

Pro Tip: For best results, you can also attach additional documents to Copilot:

  • Your CV/resume
  • The job description

Use the attachment button in Copilot to upload these files before running the prompt.

Authenticity Matters: This is a DRAFT. Add your authentic voice and verify all connections to your experience. Recruiters may use AI detection tools, compare applications with interviews, and spot unnatural language. Use AI to draft, edit, or enhance - but it is NOT you. Your authentic voice will make you stand apart.

Exercise 4: The Job Decoder - Beat the ATS

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) scan CVs for keywords before recruiters see them. Missing keywords? You're filtered out. This prompt helps you identify which keywords from the job description are missing from your CV.

The Prompt:

What are the top 5-10 keywords from this job description that are missing from my CV? For each one, tell me if it's something I should add and where it would fit.

How to use: Attach both your CV and the job description to Copilot before running this prompt. The AI will compare them and identify missing keywords.

Remember: The AI needs YOUR facts and metrics. When it suggests adding keywords, weave them naturally into your existing achievements. Transform duties into achievements using specific examples from your experience.
