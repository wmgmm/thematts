AI Productivity Exercises

Try these practical exercises to enhance your workplace productivity with AI tools. These techniques will help you learn progressively, extract actionable insights, and challenge your thinking.

Exercise 1: Progressive Learning (ELI5)

This technique helps you learn complex topics in stages, starting with simple explanations and gradually increasing in complexity. It's especially useful for mastering new concepts or technical subjects.

Workplace Application: Perfect for learning new technical skills, understanding complex processes, or preparing to explain difficult concepts to colleagues with different expertise levels.
"Teach me [TOPIC] in stages: first like I'm 5, then gradually more advanced. Ask questions after each stage to check understanding."

Replace [TOPIC] with any subject you need to learn, such as "cloud computing," "cybersecurity," "agile work," "project management," etc.

Exercise 2: Strategic Summary

This technique uses action learning principles to extract practical insights and actionable information from websites, documents, or other content sources.

Workplace Application: Use this approach to quickly process large volumes of information, identify key takeaways, and determine practical next steps from articles, reports, or industry resources.
"Using action learning, summarise this website (INSERT URL) focusing on what to do, what choices matter and what we can learn from it. Highlight any key data."

You can use this with a website URL e.g. https://www.amazon.com/ or attach a Word document, PDF, or copy and paste any text content that you need to analyze.

Exercise 3: Devil's Advocate - Uncovering Blind Spots

This exercise helps identify potential weaknesses, assumptions, or blind spots in your thinking by providing constructive criticism and alternative perspectives.

Workplace Application: Ideal for testing the robustness of proposals, plans, or decisions before finalizing them. Helps identify risks and alternatives you might not have considered.

How to Use This Exercise:

  1. Choose any scenario relevant to your work
  2. Use the initial prompt with AI
  3. Use 2-3 follow-up prompts to dig deeper
  4. Share the most surprising insight you discovered

Choose any of these workplace scenarios:

  • Your LinkedIn/CV
  • A project proposal
  • A workplace policy
  • A business decision
  • A marketing strategy

Quick Examples:

LinkedIn/CV Review: "My CV shows 3 years in customer service and 2 years in project management. I want to move into leadership."

Project Proposal: "Proposal to implement new scheduling software for our team of 15 people."

Business Decision: "Considering expanding our business hours to include weekends."

"Act as a constructive devil's advocate. First identify potential weaknesses or blind spots, then suggest alternative perspectives. Be specific and challenging but constructive. My scenario is: [scenario here]"

After getting the initial response, try these follow-up prompts to dig deeper:

"What important counterarguments am I missing?"
"What assumptions am I making that could be questioned?"
"What alternative approaches should I consider?"
"What skills or resources might I need that I haven't considered?"
"How might different stakeholders view this differently?"

Kaizen Productivity Exercises

These exercises focus on the concept of Kaizen - small, continuous improvements that can significantly enhance your workplace productivity. Try these AI-powered approaches to identify and implement incremental changes in your daily workflow.

Exercise 1: Digital Waste Identification

Kaizen begins with identifying areas of waste in your digital workflow. Use AI to help you recognize inefficiencies in how you manage your time, tasks, and digital tools.

Workplace Application: This exercise helps you pinpoint specific activities that consume your time without adding value, such as excessive email checking, inefficient file organization, or multitasking that reduces focus and effectiveness.
"I want to reduce digital waste in my workday. Please help me identify potential sources of digital waste in [specific area, e.g., email management, meeting practices, task organization]. For each type of waste, suggest a small, immediate change I could implement tomorrow to improve."
"Analyze my typical workday scenario: I check emails throughout the day, attend 4-5 meetings, work on multiple projects simultaneously, and often stay late to catch up. Using lean principles, identify 3 types of digital waste in this routine and suggest one small kaizen improvement for each."

After receiving suggestions, consider which small change feels most doable and commit to trying it for one week.

Exercise 2: Process Improvement Experiment

Kaizen emphasizes experimentation with small improvements. Use AI to help design a simple process improvement experiment that follows the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle.

Workplace Application: This structured approach helps you systematically test small changes to your work processes, measure their impact, and decide whether to adopt, modify, or discard them based on actual results.
"Help me design a one-week kaizen experiment to improve my [specific process, e.g., meeting efficiency, email processing, focus time]. Create a simple PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) framework with: 1) A specific small change to implement, 2) How I'll measure success, 3) A daily check-in process, and 4) How to evaluate and adjust after the week ends."
"I spend too much time in my inbox and feel overwhelmed by emails. Create a 5-day kaizen experiment that tests a new approach to email management. Include specific changes to implement each day, metrics to track, and a simple way to evaluate if the new approach is working."

Remember that kaizen is about continuous improvement - your experiment doesn't need to be perfect, just better than your current approach.

Exercise 3: Priority Matrix Development

Effective prioritization is key to productivity. This exercise uses AI to help you develop a personalized priority matrix for evaluating tasks and responsibilities.

Workplace Application: A customized priority matrix helps you make consistent decisions about what to focus on, what to delegate, and what to eliminate, reducing the mental load of constant reprioritization throughout your day.
"Create a personalized task priority matrix for my role as [your job title]. Include 4 quadrants with clear criteria for each. Then, take my current task list and categorize each item: [list 5-10 current tasks]. Finally, suggest a daily routine that would help me focus on the right quadrant at the right time."
"I struggle with prioritizing my work effectively. Design a simple decision-making framework I can use when new requests come in. It should help me quickly determine if a task is urgent, important, both, or neither, and guide my response accordingly. Include example scenarios to show how it works."

Try using your new priority matrix for one week, then review and refine it based on what worked and what didn't.

AI Communication Exercises

Try these practical exercises to enhance your workplace communication using AI tools. These techniques can help you overcome blank page syndrome, draft better emails, and explain complex topics simply.

Exercise 4: Brainstorming - The Initial Idea

Start with your basic idea and ask the AI for assistance in developing it. Continuous chatting with an AI can generate different, and maybe better ideas. You never need to start from a blank page!

Workplace Application: Use this technique when drafting reports, creating project plans, or developing presentation outlines. Instead of staring at a blank document, let AI help you generate an initial structure that you can refine.
"Help me brainstorm some unique social media post ideas for [type of business]."
"Help me brainstorm some key sections for our quarterly department report. I need to cover our team's achievements, challenges we faced, and plans for next quarter."

Remember: When the chatbot responds, don't be afraid to disagree, suggest changes, or ask for justification. You're in control of the final output.

Exercise 5: Giving Direction with Role Prompting

Role prompting is when you ask an AI to respond as if it were a specific type of expert or character. This shapes the AI's output to match the expertise level, terminology, and perspective you need.

Workplace Application: Use role prompting to draft emails or documents from different professional perspectives. This helps when communicating with various stakeholders who have different concerns and information needs.
"You are a [type of professional, e.g., medical professional, financial analyst, HR specialist]. Reply to the statement '[insert your specific question or statement]'."
"You are a [role, e.g., technical project manager]. Help me draft an email explaining [topic, e.g., our software deployment delay] to [audience, e.g., our development team], focusing on [specific aspects, e.g., technical challenges and next steps]."

Try the same prompt but change the role to different positions to see how the tone and content shifts.

Exercise 6: Explain It Like I'm Five (ELI5)

This technique simplifies complicated topics to an elementary level. It avoids jargon and uses analogies to give a more engaging and understandable response.

Workplace Application: Perfect for creating explanations for non-technical stakeholders, new team members, or preparing client-facing documentation that needs to be accessible.
"Explain [topic] like I'm five."
"Explain [specific workplace process or concept] like I'm five years old. After explaining, ask me questions to check my understanding and help me learn more."
"Explain [topic] with increasing complexity in three paragraphs - first for a beginner, then for someone with basic knowledge, and finally for a colleague with technical understanding."
