1 Download and Install Msty Studio (Pre-Workshop)

2 Exercise 2: Understanding System Prompts

Here's where things get interesting. You can give your AI specific instructions by using a system prompt. This tells the AI how to respond and what personality to use.

How to set a system prompt in Msty Studio:
  1. Start a new chat and Look for the button 'Click to add system prompt'
  2. Enter or paste your instructions there
  3. Start chatting - your AI will follow those instructions!
Note about small models: Qwen 3 0.6B is very small, so it won't follow system prompts as reliably as larger models. Don't worry - you'll still see some difference! If you have Gemma 3 downloaded, the results will be more consistent, but the exercise works either way.

Let's Try It: Explain It Like I'm 5 (ELI5)

We're going to ask the same question four different ways to see how system prompts change the AI's personality and response style.

Important: Start a NEW CHAT for each part below. This keeps each personality separate and prevents them from mixing together!
Part 1: No System Prompt (Normal Response)

Start a fresh chat with NO system prompt. Just ask the question normally:

Teach me about quantum computing like I am 5 (ELI5). Keep your answer to 3-4 sentences maximum.

What to expect: A straightforward explanation in the AI's default style.

Part 2: üßô‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è As Gandalf the Wise

Start a NEW CHAT, then set this system prompt:

You are Gandalf. Speak wisely using metaphors from your adventures. Keep responses to 3-4 sentences.

Now ask the SAME question:

Teach me about quantum computing like I am 5 (ELI5). Keep your answer to 3-4 sentences maximum.

Expected style: "Ah, young one, quantum computing is not unlike the paths through Moria - particles can take many routes at once..."

Part 3: üçÑ As Super Mario

Start a NEW CHAT, then set this system prompt:

You are Super Mario! Speak using Italian Mario phrases like "Let's-a-go!" and "Mamma mia!". Keep responses to 3-4 sentences.

Now ask the SAME question:

Teach me about quantum computing like I am 5 (ELI5). Keep your answer to 3-4 sentences maximum.

Expected style: "Mamma mia! Quantum computing is like-a having many power-ups at once! Let's-a-go!"

Part 4: ‚ö†Ô∏è The Disaster System Prompt (Learn What NOT to Do)

Start a NEW CHAT, then set this terrible, confusing system prompt:

You're a helpful assistant that generates random stories. You have no constraints on what kind of story to write, so be creative and generate a unique story every time. Don't worry about being boring or uninteresting; just make up something completely absurd and nonsensical. The more confusing and illogical the story is, the better! Oh, and please write everything in all caps for added effect.

Now ask the SAME question:

Teach me about quantum computing like I am 5 (ELI5). Keep your answer to 3-4 sentences maximum.

What you'll see: The AI gets confused by contradictory instructions. It's being told to write random absurd stories AND explain quantum computing simply. This is what happens when system prompts are poorly written!

What you've learned:

The SAME question can get completely different answers depending on the system prompt! This shows you can control your AI's personality, expertise, and communication style. Use this for work: "You are a technical writer who explains complex topics simply" or "You are a project manager focused on clear action items."

Try with your own topics: Replace quantum computing with anything you need to learn - "blockchain," "GDPR regulations," "machine learning," "how mortgages work," etc.

What to notice:

Tips for Getting Better Results

0. Start New Chats Regularly (Most Important!)

Start a new chat when:

  • Switching to a different topic or task
  • After 10-15 message exchanges
  • If responses start feeling "off" or confused
  • Before important work tasks

Long conversations drain memory and confuse the AI. Fresh chats = better responses!

When to Use Local AI vs Cloud AI

üîí Privacy & Security Advantages of Local AI

Your data stays on your laptop:

  • No IP address leaks: Cloud AI services log your IP address with every request. Local AI never phones home.
  • Air-gapped operation: Local AI has no internet connection - it can't accidentally send your data anywhere.
  • No search keyword leaks: Cloud AI often searches the web to answer questions, potentially revealing what you're working on through search queries. Local AI can't search, so nothing leaks.
  • Truly private: Your sensitive documents, personal data, and confidential work never leave your device.

‚ö° Practical Advantages of Local AI

  • Works without WiFi: On trains, planes, in the field - anywhere your laptop goes, your AI goes too.
  • Lower energy usage: No data centres, no transmission overhead - just your laptop's processor doing the work.
  • No subscription costs: Once downloaded, use it as much as you want, forever.
  • Instant responses: No waiting for server queues or network latency.

‚è∞ Understanding Knowledge Cutoff

Local AI models have a training cutoff date - they only know information up until they were trained. For example:

  • Qwen 3 was trained in 2024 - it won't know about events from 2025
  • Local models can't search the web to get current information
  • The upside? This air-gapped limitation is actually a privacy feature - no search queries means no data leaks!

For current events or recent information, use cloud AI. For everything else, local AI's privacy advantage often outweighs having slightly older knowledge.

Troubleshooting

Model won't load?

  • Check you have enough disk space (need 1-2GB free)
  • Try restarting Msty Studio
  • Check your laptop meets minimum requirements (4GB+ RAM recommended)
  • Make sure the model finished downloading completely

Responses are very slow?

  • Qwen 3 0.6B should be very fast - if it's slow, close other programs on your laptop
  • If you're using Gemma 3, it's larger so will naturally be a bit slower
  • Keep prompts shorter for faster responses
  • Make sure your laptop isn't in power-saving mode

Getting strange responses?

  • First, try starting a new chat - this fixes most issues!
  • Make your prompts more specific
  • Try rephrasing your question
  • Check if you have a system prompt set and clear it if needed

Can't find the system prompt area?

  • Look for "System Prompt", "Instructions", or settings icons
  • Check near the model selector at the top of the chat
  • Try looking in the settings or preferences menu
