Exercise 3 Let AI Write Prompts for You Copilot
What you are doing: Getting Copilot to generate ready-to-use prompts tailored to a repetitive task from your role.
Paste this prompt into Copilot, then describe any repetitive task. Copilot will generate 3 ready-to-use prompts.
"Act as a Copilot Productivity expert. Start by asking: "What repetitive task would you like help with?" and wait for a reply. After the user replies: Ask 1 clarifying question (only if needed) and wait for their reply. Provide 3 ready-to-use Copilot AI prompts. Rules: Prompts must rely only on pasted text. No email, calendar, or app access. Skip tasks AI genuinely cannot do."

Try: "I spend a lot of time writing meeting summaries" or "I categorise customer feedback emails every Monday".

Exercise 4 Try Creating a Simple Agent (Optional) Copilot
What you are doing: Building a small, reusable Copilot assistant to see how agents work - no coding required.
Agents are reusable Copilot assistants with a specific focus. Build one once, use it every day, and share it with your team.
  1. Open Copilot - Go to m365copilot.com and click "Create agent" in the left-hand menu.
  2. Enter the Description - In the "Describe" tab, type the following and hit the submit arrow:
    "The user will provide a word and you must generate 3 possible crossword style clues for that word"
  3. Click Create - You may be prompted to name your agent. Reply to that, then click Create.
  4. Click Go to Agent - Wait for the success message, then click the blue "Go to Agent" button.
  5. Test It - Type a single word like "Book" and check it generates 3 crossword clues.
Exercise 5 Process Mapping Your Work No Copilot
What you are doing: Mapping a real task from your role to understand:
  1. What actually happens
  2. Where it hurts or slows down
  3. Where AI or automation might help

Steps

One action per note

Problems

Delays, errors, frustration

Automate

Write "PA", "AI", or "PA+AI"

1

Map the Steps

Write every action on a yellow note, left to right. Map what actually happens, not what you wish happened.

2

Find the Problems

Stick a pink note next to any step that causes delays, errors, or frustration. These show where time, effort, or patience is being wasted.

Where do you wait?

Approvals, replies, access

Where do mistakes happen?

Wrong data, duplicated effort

What is boring or repetitive?

Copy-paste, chasing people

What takes way too long?

Low value for time spent

Clusters of pink = process hotspots. Biggest wins come from fixing these.
3

Decide How Technology Might Help

Look only at the pink problem steps. Ask one question:

"Is this problem caused by repetition, rules, or thinking?"

PA

Predictable work

Same steps, moving data, reminders, clear rules

AI

Cognitive work

Reading, writing, summarising, answering questions

PA+AI

Trigger + judgement

PA kicks it off, AI does the thinking

⚠️ Not every problem should be automated. If fixing it would only save seconds or add risk, leave it alone.

Hold onto your map. You will use it in Exercise 6.

Exercise 6 Copilot Reads Your Process Map Copilot
What you are doing: Uploading a photo of your process map so Copilot can suggest where AI might help across your workflow.
📱

Step 1

Take a photo of your process map from Exercise 5.

📤

Step 2

Open a New Chat in Copilot, click the attachment icon, and upload your photo.

💬

Step 3

Paste the prompt below alongside your image.

"Read the specific tasks written on the sticky notes in this image. Based only on those tasks, write 3 ready-to-use Microsoft Copilot Chat prompts to speed up the repetitive work. Rule: The prompts must only work with text the user pastes into the chat. No email, calendar, or app integrations allowed."
