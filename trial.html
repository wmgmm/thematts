Exercises

Hands-on activities to explore Copilot's capabilities.

Exercise 1 ‚Äì My First M365 Prompt (The Roast)

Test the Connection: Let's verify Copilot can see your specific data by asking for a "Roast". If it mocks you for that specific 8am meeting next Tuesday, you know it's working.

"For a script: Analyze my actual calendar for the next 7 days with specifics and roast it."

Note: This demonstrates Graph Connection (reading your calendar), it's best if you have GPT-5 Enabled.

Exercise 3 ‚Äì The "Catch Up" Challenge (Broad vs. Targeted)

Part A (The "Broad" Way): First, let's try a standard natural language request. Observe how Copilot has to "guess" what is important to you across your whole inbox.

"I've been away for a week. Summarize my missed emails and Teams messages and tell me what is urgent."

Part B (The "Pro" Way): Now, use the / (Slash) to anchor Copilot. Replace [Manager Name] with your actual boss or a close colleague.

"I've been away for a week. Summarize all communication from /[Manager Name] and highlight any direct questions they asked me."

Why use the / ? Typing a name normally is just a fuzzy search (looking for the text). Using / creates a hard link to the specific user profile, ensuring Copilot ignores accidental name matches and looks at the correct source.

üí° Pro Tip: As seen in the menu, the forward slash / isn't just for People. You can anchor to Files, Meetings, Chats, and Sites. This forces Copilot to ignore the noise and focus strictly on the data source you select.

Exercise 4 ‚Äì Excel Integration

Open Excel and try these prompts in sequence:

Step 1: Create Data

"Create a table of 50 fictitious employees with columns for Name, Department, Salary, and Hire Date."

Step 2: Calculate Years of Service

"Add a new column that calculates 'Years of Service' based on the Hire Date and today's date."

Step 3: Add Bonus Column

"Add a column called 'Bonus'. If the Department is Sales, the bonus is 10% of Salary. For everyone else, it is 5%."

Step 4: Generate Emails

"Add a column called 'Email' that combines the first letter of their name and their full last name @company.com, all in lowercase."

Step 5: Department Summary

"Create a new sheet showing the total salary cost breakdown by Department."

Step 6: Highlight Top Earners

"Highlight the top 10% of earners in Red."

Step 7: Data Labels

"Add data labels to the line so I can see the exact number for each year."

More Copilot Chat Examples

Advanced prompts that combine multiple M365 data sources.

Conflict Resolution

Uses semantic search to find conflict (not just keywords), saving you hours of cross-referencing threads.

"Search my recent Teams chats and emails with [Person A] and [Person B] from the last 2 weeks. List every instance where they gave conflicting instructions on [Project Name] and summarize the discrepancy in a table."
Audit Trail

Acts as a forensic tool, finding the exact metadata of a past action to use as evidence in a new action.

"I need to prove we sent the deliverables. Find the specific email where I sent the final [Project] PDF to [Client Name] last month, and draft a follow-up email that references the date and time of that original message."
Meeting Prep

Filters specifically for incoming attachments from a specific person, ignoring your own internal noise.

"I have a meeting with [Customer/Stakeholder] tomorrow. Surface the last 3 files they shared with me via email or Teams, and summarize the 'Next Steps' slide or section from each."
Commitment Tracking

Connects two data points: the promise made in a transcript and the lack of a confirmation signal in the chat.

"Scan all meeting transcripts from [Project Alpha] series in the last month. List every action item assigned to me that does not yet have a corresponding 'Done' or 'Completed' status update in the chat."
Pricing History

Turns unstructured email history into structured data (a price trend analysis) without you opening a single PDF.

"Check my email history for quotes received from [Vendor X] over the last two years. Extract the total price and line-item cost for [Service Y] from each email and plot the price increase trend."
Contract Review (Word)

Compares two documents (the open file vs. the reference file) and performs a specific redlining task.

"Review this MSA against our standard /[Risk_Framework.docx]. Comment on every clause that exceeds our standard liability cap, and suggest a redline edit that aligns with the 'Low Risk' definition in the framework."
Root Cause (Excel)

Uses the Python in Excel integration to perform clustering analysis that standard Excel formulas cannot do.

"Analyze this error log. Use Python to cluster the failure types by time of day and machine ID. Identify which single machine is responsible for the majority of 'Type B' errors and visualize the correlation."
Slide Mirroring (PowerPoint)

Solves the biggest pain point of AI slides (bad formatting) by forcing it to "clone" the style of an existing, approved slide.

"Create a new slide for 'Q4 Strategy' that mirrors the exact layout, font size, and visual style of slide 3 in /[Q3_Review.pptx], but populate it with the bullet points from /[Q4_Memo.docx]."
Inbox Triage (Outlook)

Uses the Agent capability to perform an action (Delete) and a filter (Deadline search) simultaneously.

"Review the 50 unread emails in my 'Notifications' folder. Delete anything that is a system alert or newsletter. For the rest, summarize the 3 that require a reply today based on deadline keywords like 'ASAP' or 'by Friday'."
Knowledge Transfer (Teams)

Uses "Graph Signal" (activity/popularity) to determine what is "important" rather than just giving you a list of everything.

"I am onboarding @[New Hire] to [Project X]. Create a 'Read Me' list by finding the 5 most active documents and the 3 most important decision threads from our team channel in the last 6 months."
Useful Symbols

Key symbols to power up your prompts.

Symbol Name Function Example
/ The Grounding Key Opens the menu to insert Files, Meetings, or Emails as context. "Summarize /[File Name]"
@ The Agent Key Invokes specific "Agents" (Skills/Apps). "@Web find the latest news on..."
" " The Anchor Forces an "Exact Phrase" search (stops fuzzy guessing). "Find emails about 'Project Falcon'"
Work vs Web Mode

Choose the right mode for your question.

üü¢
Work Mode (The Green Shield)

What it sees: Your emails, files, Teams, Calendar

Privacy: Total. Your data never leaves the company.

Use when: The answer is inside your company.

"Catch me up on the Project Alpha email thread."
üåê
Web Mode (The Globe)

What it sees: The Internet (News, Stocks, Wikipedia, Public Info)

Privacy: Protected. Microsoft doesn't save it, but don't paste secrets.

Use when: The answer is outside your company.

"What are the latest trends in AI for 2025?"
üí° The "Web Content" Toggle: Found inside Work Mode. When ON, Work Mode is allowed to "peek" at Google/Bing to add outside context (e.g. stock prices) to your internal search.
